Common Pest Problems in Greenville, NC

Greenville, like many areas, is prone to a variety of common household and commercial pests. Understanding the signs of these infestations is the first step in seeking professional help. Here are some of the prevalent pest issues we regularly address:

Ant Infestations

Ants are a ubiquitous problem, often entering homes in search of food and water. While individually harmless, large colonies can be a significant nuisance and contaminate food sources. Seeing trails of ants marching across your countertops or floors is a clear indicator of an infestation. We can identify the ant species and apply targeted treatments to eliminate the colony and prevent their return.

Rodent Activity

Mice and rats are not only unsightly but can also cause significant damage to property by gnawing on wires and structures. They also carry diseases that can be harmful to humans. Signs of rodent activity include droppings, gnaw marks, and scratching noises in walls or ceilings. Our rodent control services involve trapping, baiting, and exclusion to remove the pests and seal entry points.

Cockroach Problems

Cockroaches are resilient and can quickly multiply, posing serious health risks due to the bacteria and allergens they carry. Seeing a cockroach, dead or alive, is a strong indication of an infestation. We employ effective methods to eliminate cockroaches and implement preventative measures to keep them from returning.

Termite Damage

Termites are silent destroyers, often causing extensive damage to the structure of a building before their presence is even noticed. Signs of termites include mud tubes on walls, discarded wings near windowsills, or hollow-sounding wood. Our termite treatments are designed to eliminate existing colonies and protect your property from future infestations, requiring specialized expertise and ongoing monitoring.

Bed Bug Infestations

Bed bugs are notoriously difficult to eliminate and can cause itchy, uncomfortable bites. They are often brought into homes through travel or used furniture. Finding small, reddish-brown insects or tiny bloodstains on your bedding are indicators of a bed bug problem. Our bed bug extermination services are thorough and effective, targeting these elusive pests in all stages of their lifecycle and we understand the importance of swift and precise action when dealing with bed bugs, given their ability to hide in unexpected places and their rapid reproduction rate. Rest assured, our methods are designed to be as minimally disruptive as possible while achieving complete eradication. After initial treatment, follow-up inspections are typically recommended to ensure no bed bugs remain, offering you continued peace of mind.

Spider Concerns

While most spiders are harmless, some species can be venomous and pose a threat to your family. Even non-venomous spiders can be unsettling for many people. Excessive cobwebs around your property or seeing spiders frequently are signs you may need professional spider removal. Our technicians can identify dangerous species and implement strategies to reduce the spider population around your home, including treating nesting areas and sealing entry points to prevent their ingress. We also offer advice on how to make your property less attractive to spiders.

Flea and Tick Control

Fleas and ticks are not only bothersome to your pets but can also transmit diseases to humans. Signs of a flea infestation include excessive scratching in pets or small, dark insects jumping on carpets or furniture. Ticks are often found in wooded areas or tall grass and can attach to pets and people. Our flea and tick control services target these parasites in your home and yard, creating a safer environment for your family and furry companions. This often involves treating indoor areas where pets frequent and outdoor areas where ticks and fleas are likely to reside. We take a comprehensive approach to break the flea and tick lifecycle.

Bee and Wasp Removal

Bees and wasps can be a major concern, especially for individuals with allergies. Attempting to remove a nest yourself can be dangerous. Seeing an abundance of these stinging insects around your property or locating a visible nest indicates a need for professional removal. Our technicians are trained to safely and effectively remove bee and wasp nests, minimizing the risk of stings. We prioritize safety for both our technicians and your family during this process, and we aim to remove the nest with minimal disturbance to the surrounding environment. We always assess the situation carefully before proceeding with removal.

Mosquito Control

Mosquitoes are not only annoying but also pose a health risk as they can transmit diseases like West Nile virus. Enjoying your outdoor spaces can be difficult with a large mosquito population. Our mosquito control services target breeding grounds and resting areas to significantly reduce the number of mosquitoes on your property, allowing you to enjoy your yard again. This often involves identifying and treating standing water sources and applying treatments to vegetation where mosquitoes hide. Our goal is to create a mosquito-free zone for you and your family.

Silverfish Control

Silverfish may not be harmful to humans, but they can cause damage to books, paper, and clothing. These nocturnal insects are often found in damp, dark areas. Seeing silverfish scuttling across floors or finding evidence of their feeding (irregularly shaped holes in paper) suggests an infestation. We can implement strategies to eliminate silverfish and address the moisture issues that attract them, including dehumidification and targeted treatments in areas where they are active. We also advise on proper storage of vulnerable items.

Tick Control

As mentioned with flea control, ticks are a serious concern due to their ability to transmit debilitating diseases like Lyme disease. Protecting your family and pets from ticks is paramount. Our tick control services focus on treating outdoor areas where ticks are prevalent, such as tall grass, brush, and wooded edges. We aim to reduce the tick population on your property and provide tips on how to avoid tick bites and conduct tick checks. Regular yard maintenance can also play a significant role in tick prevention.

Moth Control

Certain types of moths can cause damage to clothing, carpets, and stored food items. Identifying the specific type of moth is important for effective treatment. Seeing moths flying around your home, finding larvae or damaged items are signs of an infestation. Our moth control services involve identifying the source of the infestation and implementing targeted treatments to eliminate moths in all life stages. We also provide advice on proper storage to prevent future problems. We understand that some moth infestations can be particularly stubborn.

Wildlife Control

Beyond insects and rodents, our services extend to wildlife control for common nuisance animals like squirrels and raccoons. Squirrels can cause damage by gnawing on structures and wires, while raccoons can be destructive and carry diseases. Our wildlife control methods are humane and prioritize the safe removal of these animals from your property and prevention of their return. We also offer services for residential and commercial properties, including routine pest management plans to keep your property protected year-round. When to Call a Professional Exterminator

Knowing when to call a professional for pest control is crucial for protecting your property and your health. While DIY pest control methods might seem appealing in the short term, they often only address the surface of the problem and can even make infestations worse. Here are some clear signs that it's time to contact Wilson Pest Pros:

Persistent Pest Sightings

If you are consistently seeing the same type of pest, no matter how many times you try to eliminate them yourself, it's a strong indication that you have a more significant infestation than you can handle with over-the-counter products. Pests reproduce quickly, and what seems like a few can rapidly become a large and stubborn problem. Professional exterminators have access to more powerful treatments and the expertise to locate and eliminate the source of the infestation, unlike many common sprays that only affect the pests you can see. They can also identify potential breeding grounds that you might overlook, preventing future generations from emerging.

Evidence of Nesting or Breeding

Finding nests, droppings, eggs, or larvae is a definitive sign of a breeding population of pests on your property. These are clear indicators that the infestation is established and actively growing. Trying to remove a nest yourself can be dangerous, especially with pests like bees, wasps, or rodents. Professional exterminators are equipped with the proper safety gear and knowledge to safely and effectively remove nests and disrupt breeding cycles, protecting you from potential harm and ensuring the complete eradication of the infestation. They understand the specific nesting habits of different pests and can target these areas precisely.

Property Damage

If you notice unexplained damage to your home or business, pests could be the culprits. Gnaw marks on wood or wires, holes in walls, damaged insulation, or structural damage can all be signs of pest activity, particularly from rodents, termites, or carpenter ants. Ignoring these signs can lead to costly repairs down the line. A professional inspection can identify the pest responsible for the damage and develop a plan to eliminate the pest and prevent further destruction, potentially saving you a significant amount of money in the long run. Delaying professional intervention can allow the damage to worsen considerably.

Health Concerns

Many pests carry diseases and allergens that can pose serious health risks to humans and pets. If you notice an increase in allergies, unexplained bites or rashes, or if someone in your household has a compromised immune system, a pest infestation could be the cause. Pest droppings, urine, and shed exoskeletons can all contribute to poor indoor air quality and trigger respiratory issues. Professional pest control can eliminate these health hazards and create a safer living environment for you and your family, providing peace of mind regarding your health and well-being. Don't underestimate the potential health implications of a pest problem.

Difficulty Identifying the Pest

Sometimes, you may see signs of pest activity but are unsure what type of pest you're dealing with. Different pests require different treatment methods, and misidentifying the pest can lead to ineffective treatments and wasted time and money. Professional exterminators are trained to accurately identify various pests and recommend the most appropriate and effective course of action. They can also educate you on the specific pest and how to prevent future encounters, empowering you with knowledge. Proper identification is the cornerstone of effective pest management.

Infestation is Widespread

If you are seeing pests in multiple areas of your home or business, it's a sign that the infestation is widespreadand likely requires a professional approach. A localized problem can often be dealt with more easily, but when pests have spread throughout the property, it indicates a larger, more established population that needs comprehensive treatment. Professional exterminators have the resources and expertise to treat your entire property effectively, ensuring all affected areas are addressed and eradicating the infestation from every corner of your home or business. A widespread infestation requires a strategic and thorough approach.

You Feel Uncomfortable or Unsafe

Ultimately, if you feel uncomfortable or unsafe in your own home or business due to the presence of pests, it's time to call in the professionals. Your peace of mind is important, and living with a pest problem can be incredibly stressful and anxiety-inducing. Professional pest control provides not only effective elimination of pests but also the reassurance and peace of mind that comes with knowing the problem is being handled by experts. Don't hesitate to seek professional help if the situation is impacting your well-being. Feeling safe and comfortable in your environment is essential.
